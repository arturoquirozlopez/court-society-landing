A private tennis network. Curated members. Founders, executives, and investors. Tennis access at home and on the road.

Court Society introduces members to trusted players in the cities they visit. Membership is by nomination. The Society is small on purpose, and is meant to feel that way.

How membership works
01
Nomination.
Membership is by nomination, from a current Member or a respected source. A small number of applications are accepted directly.
02
Application.
A short, considered application. The Steward’s Office reads every one.
03
Review.
Applications are reviewed against the Society’s standards for caliber, fit, and tennis substance.
04
Acceptance.
A decision arrives within seven days. Accepted members are received into the next cohort.
05
Introductions.
New members are introduced to players in their home city — and to players in the cities they travel to most.

What Members find

A small set of quiet privileges.

Trusted players
Vetted matches in every active city, prepared by the Steward’s Office before you land.
City Houses
A small, hosted group in each active city — Santiago House, São Paulo House, Miami House — run by a local Member.
Club ecosystem
Access to the clubs Members already belong to, through Member-hosts in each city.
Curated introductions
Concierge matchmaking by hand. No algorithm. Every introduction carries a name behind it.
Matches
Singles and doubles, social or competitive, by request — at home or in the cities you travel to most.
Rankings
A private member ladder, updated quietly. For those who like to know.
Events
Monthly Court Sessions, Founders’ Mornings, and the occasional weekend tournament. Always small, always considered.
